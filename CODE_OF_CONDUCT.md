# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers pledge to make participation in the Eventix open-source community a harassment-free experience for everyone. We are committed to creating a welcoming, inclusive, and respectful environment, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior
- Demonstrate respect and consideration for others.
- Communicate openly and constructively.
- Accept and provide feedback gracefully.
- Show empathy towards other community members.
- Use inclusive and professional language.
- Collaborate with the intent to improve the project and community.

### Unacceptable Behavior
- Harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, or inflammatory discussions.
- Publishing othersâ€™ private information without permission.
- Threats of violence or encouraging self-harm.
- Sustained disruption of discussions or project activities.
- Sexualized language or imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct. They may:
- Take appropriate corrective or disciplinary action.
- Remove comments, code, or contributions that violate this policy.
- Temporarily or permanently ban contributors for repeated or severe violations.

## Reporting Issues

If you observe or experience behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, report it confidentially to the Eventix maintainers.

All reports will be reviewed promptly and handled with confidentiality and fairness.

## Enforcement Guidelines

### 1. Correction
**Consequence:** A private, written warning.  
Used for minor, one-time incidents or misunderstandings.

### 2. Warning
**Consequence:** A clear warning and temporary exclusion from interactions.  
Used when behavior repeats or impacts others.

### 3. Temporary Ban
**Consequence:** Temporary removal from participation in project spaces.  
Used for serious violations or repeated misconduct.

### 4. Permanent Ban
**Consequence:** Permanent removal from community participation.  
Used for severe, harmful, or repeated violations with no improvement.
